END-TO-END AV AGENT RESEARCH

Classic modular stack:
sensing -> perception -> localization -> prediction -> planning -> control

End-to-end AV agents try to learn a larger part of that stack in one model. For example, a model may take camera images, LiDAR, sensor history, map or route context, and a navigation command, then output a future trajectory, waypoints, or driving actions.

Well-known and recent research examples students may hear about:
- TransFuser: transformer-based camera and LiDAR fusion for driving.
- UniAD: a unified planning-oriented model connecting perception, prediction, and planning.
- VAD: vectorized scene representation for efficient autonomous-driving planning.
- DriveLM-Agent: a vision-language driving agent that reasons from perception toward planning.
- OpenDriveVLA and DriveAgent-R1: newer vision-language-action style AV-agent research.
- EMMA: Waymo research using a multimodal model to predict driving trajectories from camera and route context.

Key idea: end-to-end models are powerful research tools, but real safety-critical AV systems still need careful evaluation, simulation, fallback logic, maps, monitoring, and engineering around the model.
